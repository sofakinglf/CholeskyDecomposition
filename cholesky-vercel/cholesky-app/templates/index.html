Topic #09

Cholesky Decomposition

Discussion Examples Calculator
01

Mathematical Discussion

Cholesky decomposition factorises a symmetric positive-definite (SPD) matrix $A$ into the product of a lower-triangular matrix $L$ and its transpose $L^T$:

$$A = L\,L^T$$

It is roughly twice as efficient as LU decomposition for SPD matrices, exploiting symmetry to halve arithmetic work, and is numerically unconditionally stable — no pivoting required.

Required Conditions

C1

Square: $A$ is $n \times n$.

C2

Symmetric: $a_{ij} = a_{ji}$ for all $i,j$.

C3

Positive-definite: $\mathbf{x}^T A\mathbf{x} > 0$ for all non-zero $\mathbf{x}$. Equivalently, all eigenvalues are positive.

—

Derivation of Formulas

Expanding $A = LL^T$ element-by-element: $a_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{\min(i,j)} l_{ik}\,l_{jk}$. Solving column by column:

Diagonal entry $(i = j)$: $$l_{jj} = \sqrt{a_{jj} - \sum_{k=1}^{j-1} l_{jk}^2}$$
Sub-diagonal entry $(i > j)$: $$l_{ij} = \frac{1}{l_{jj}}\!\left(a_{ij} - \sum_{k=1}^{j-1} l_{ik}\,l_{jk}\right)$$
Complexity

$O(n^3/3)$ — roughly half of Gaussian elimination on a general matrix.

Uniqueness

Unique when all diagonal entries of $L$ are required to be positive.

Stability

Unconditionally stable for SPD matrices. No pivoting needed.

Applications

Solving $Ax=b$, Monte Carlo sampling, least-squares, finite-element methods.

—

Solving $Ax=b$ via Cholesky

Two-Phase Back/Forward Substitution

01

Forward substitution — solve $L\mathbf{y}=\mathbf{b}$: $$y_i = \frac{1}{l_{ii}}\!\left(b_i - \sum_{k=1}^{i-1}l_{ik}\,y_k\right)$$

02

Backward substitution — solve $L^T\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{y}$: $$x_i = \frac{1}{l_{ii}}\!\left(y_i - \sum_{k=i+1}^{n}l_{ki}\,x_k\right)$$

02

Worked Examples

1

Decompose a 3×3 SPD Matrix

Compute $L$ such that $A = LL^T$, then verify.

Given
$$A = \begin{bmatrix}4&2&-2\\2&10&2\\-2&2&5\end{bmatrix}$$
Step 1

Column $j=1$

$$l_{11}=\sqrt{a_{11}}=\sqrt{4}=2$$ $$l_{21}=\frac{a_{21}}{l_{11}}=\frac{2}{2}=1 \qquad l_{31}=\frac{a_{31}}{l_{11}}=\frac{-2}{2}=-1$$
Step 2

Column $j=2$

$$l_{22}=\sqrt{a_{22}-l_{21}^2}=\sqrt{10-1}=\sqrt{9}=3$$ $$l_{32}=\frac{a_{32}-l_{31}l_{21}}{l_{22}}=\frac{2-(-1)(1)}{3}=\frac{3}{3}=1$$
Step 3

Column $j=3$

$$l_{33}=\sqrt{a_{33}-l_{31}^2-l_{32}^2}=\sqrt{5-1-1}=\sqrt{3}\approx1.7321$$
Result
$$L=\begin{bmatrix}2&0&0\\1&3&0\\-1&1&\sqrt{3}\end{bmatrix}$$

Verification:

$$LL^T=\begin{bmatrix}2&0&0\\1&3&0\\-1&1&\sqrt3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}2&1&-1\\0&3&1\\0&0&\sqrt3\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}4&2&-2\\2&10&2\\-2&2&5\end{bmatrix}=A\checkmark$$
2

Solve $Ax=b$ Using Cholesky Factorisation

Full factorisation + forward/backward substitution.

Given
$$A=\begin{bmatrix}25&15&-5\\15&18&0\\-5&0&11\end{bmatrix},\quad\mathbf{b}=\begin{bmatrix}35\\33\\6\end{bmatrix}$$
Step 1

Cholesky Factorisation

$$l_{11}=\sqrt{25}=5$$ $$l_{21}=\tfrac{15}{5}=3,\quad l_{31}=\tfrac{-5}{5}=-1$$ $$l_{22}=\sqrt{18-9}=\sqrt{9}=3$$ $$l_{32}=\tfrac{0-(-1)(3)}{3}=1$$ $$l_{33}=\sqrt{11-1-1}=\sqrt{9}=3$$
$$L=\begin{bmatrix}5&0&0\\3&3&0\\-1&1&3\end{bmatrix}$$
Step 2

Forward substitution $L\mathbf{y}=\mathbf{b}$

$$y_1=\tfrac{35}{5}=7$$ $$y_2=\tfrac{33-3(7)}{3}=\tfrac{12}{3}=4$$ $$y_3=\tfrac{6-(-1)(7)-(1)(4)}{3}=\tfrac{9}{3}=3$$
Step 3

Backward substitution $L^T\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{y}$

$$x_3=\tfrac{3}{3}=1$$ $$x_2=\tfrac{4-(1)(1)}{3}=1$$ $$x_1=\tfrac{7-(3)(1)-(-1)(1)}{5}=\tfrac{5}{5}=1$$
Solution
$$\mathbf{x}=\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}$$

Check: $25(1)+15(1)-5(1)=35\ \checkmark\quad 15(1)+18(1)+0=33\ \checkmark\quad -5(1)+0+11(1)=6\ \checkmark$
